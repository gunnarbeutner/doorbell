# V4.1 verification evidence

This is a historical evidence record for the assembled V4.1 PCB. It is not a reusable verification
procedure and must not be treated as proof for a mechanically or electrically changed revision.
The reusable pre-fab procedure is `VERIFICATION.md`.

## Bench and field results

- The JLCPCB-assembled board fits and operates in the original WF26 enclosure. The board seats on
  the bosses, the lid closes over the populated height, the transducer and wire entry align, and
  both housing buttons actuate their board switches.
- Local power measured 4.8 V after D4 and 3.3 V at TP2. The board booted and joined Wi-Fi. All four
  SSRs switched with their expected NO/NC idle states; SW3 and SW4 operated both required poles.
- Codec bias measured TP4=3.3 V and TP8/VMID=1.65 V. A test tone produced a clean DAC output.
- The passive latch pulled in and sealed on the emulated bus. It released from the board door drive
  and from a falling P2 supply. Both optocoupler ring channels detected with the assembled polarity.
- Automated pull-in attempts, classified from OC1/P4 after the external ring source opened but before
  P2 was interrupted, gave 0/5 at 8.5 V and 5/5 at 9.0 V at room temperature. This bounds the
  observed V4.1 threshold between those values; it does not replace the relay's 9.6 V guaranteed
  must-operate limit or qualify another board revision/load.
- Door-open break-before-make measured approximately 33–34 ms from P4 falling (K5 released) to P3
  rising to P2 (K2 made), with no premature P3 pulse. Scope:
  [`../scope/door-break-before-make.png`](../scope/door-break-before-make.png) (CH1=P4, CH2=P3,
  both relative to P1, 10×, 5 V/div, 10 ms/div, CH1 6 V falling trigger).
- With the door command held continuously, the hardware watchdog released the door bridge after
  approximately 6–7 seconds, safely after the normal 1.75-second firmware pulse. After 250 ms with
  DOOR_DRV low, reassertion reproduced the normal 33–34 ms P4-fall→P3-rise delay, proving that the K2
  delay path functionally re-armed; P3 then remained at approximately 12 V for 5.7–5.8 s before the
  watchdog released it. Scope:
  [`../scope/door-watchdog-rearm-250ms.png`](../scope/door-watchdog-rearm-250ms.png) (CH1=P4,
  CH2=P3, both relative to P1, 10×, 5 V/div, 1 s/div, CH2 6 V rising trigger). A 500 ms repeat
  produced the same approximately 5.8 s timeout, closing the external functional re-arm check.
- Chime suppression passed electrically and acoustically on the tone rig while both ring sensors
  remained clean.
- K3 trapped-charge reclose passed ten commanded cycles with waits from 0–5 s and zero K5
  re-latches. A separate fail-safe test cut only the board's USB power while the 12 V emulated bus
  remained live: K3's hardware-default reclose produced a brief P4 pulse peaking around 5–6 V,
  falling immediately to roughly 2 V and decaying within about 300 ms, with no 12 V seal-in. Scope:
  [`../scope/k3-usb-power-loss.png`](../scope/k3-usb-power-loss.png) (CH1=P4 relative to P1, 10×,
  5 V/div, 100 ms/div, 2 V rising trigger).
- TX produced approximately 0.55 Vrms at 1 kHz on floating P3 with PTT on. RX delivered 240 mVpp at
  MIC1P from approximately 1.5 Vpp on P2 and measured −25.7 dBFS against a −79 dBFS analog floor.
- The installed board reliably rings, seals in, opens the door and operates in normal service on
  the real TV20/S system.

## Ordered-part checks closed after the original review

- D14 BAT54SW, LCSC C22466373: SOT-323 pin 1=A/GND, pin 2=K/+3V3,
  pin 3=COM/MIC1P.
- Q3/Q4 AO3400A, LCSC C20917: SOT-23 pin 1=G, pin 2=S, pin 3=D. The manufacturer sheet specifies
  Vgs(th)=0.65–1.45 V and Igss=±100 nA.

## Prefab-review warning disposition

- IC-pin clamp edges: accepted only with codec-before-PTT sequencing; optional clamp-rail hardening
  remains available.
- D1 held-floor-call stress: open measurement because available P4 captures disagree.
- K5 pull-in: V4.1 room-temperature boundary measured between 8.5 and 9.0 V. The real ring bus is
  approximately 12 V versus the relay's 9.6 V guaranteed must-operate limit; V4.2 adds only the
  negligible 100 kΩ R36 load. Treat latch/seal-in as routine first-board bring-up, not a low-voltage
  fabrication gate.
- Door retrigger: 250 ms off restores the external break-before-make assertion sequence and a
  5.7–5.8 s watchdog interval; 500 ms produces the same result. The retained 0.5 s firmware minimum
  is externally validated.
- Watchdog: V4.1 bench pass at approximately 6–7 s; contamination/humidity and other revisions
  require their own measurement.
- LDO capacitance, LP5907 PSRR headroom, U2 thermal behavior and codec rail decay: retain as bench
  gates for subsequent boards.
- SSR LED drive: direct ESP32-S3 drive is field-proven for every actuator, including the dual-LED
  PTT and door outputs, with V4.1's 300 Ω series resistors. V4.2 uses 220 Ω for additional
  operate-current margin; no buffer stage or separate fabrication gate is required.
- D10/J3: accepted with keyed-harness polarity control; a current-limited reversed source may not
  clear the fuse.
- Codec/PTT click: codec output must settle before K1 closes and K1 must open before codec shutdown.
- D6 orientation: visually verify CPL/first-article orientation because datasheet terminal numbering
  differs from the footprint's pad-1 convention.

## Documentation corrections made after the review

Current-design descriptions use R26 for the codec TX resistor; historical V4.1 measurements that
name R28 remain historical facts. Simulator comments were updated to the later 10 MΩ watchdog
value, and the floor-call notes now correctly describe direct per-channel optocoupler emitter
returns rather than a shared emitter resistor.
