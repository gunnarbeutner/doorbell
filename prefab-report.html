B1 Codec TX output (ES8311 OUTP) is driven past its abs-max on every push-to-talk — no series current-limit

Found by: absmax:ES8311 adversarial refuter (severity = blocker, high confidence) · Independently confirmed against the netlist · Touches DESIGN.md “Audio path” (TX front-end) + the /ES_OUTP net
What the netlist actually does

The codec DAC output couples to the bus with no resistor in between: ES8311 OUTP (pin 12) → C14 (1 µF) → TALK_BRIDGE. The dual talk-SSR K1 ties TALK_BRIDGE directly to the +12 V P2 standing rail (Ron ≈ 1 Ω) whenever talk is engaged. The /ES_OUTP net contains only U3.12 and C14.1 — there is no series resistance between the codec pin and the coupling cap.

The failure

When K1 closes, TALK_BRIDGE steps from ~0 V to +9.4 … +12 V. C14 cannot change instantly, so the OUTP pin is kicked to ~+11 … +13.6 V. The ES8311's on-chip ESD clamp pins OUTP at ≈ AVDD+0.7 V (~4 V) and must sink the C14 charge current — limited only by K1's Ron and the SSR's optical turn-on rate (≈ tens–120 mA, ~10–12 µC per edge). That exceeds the ES8311 analog absolute-max (AGND−0.3 … AVDD+0.3 = −0.3 … +3.6 V, datasheet rev 17 p.9) and repeats on every PTT make and release. This is a normal-operation abs-max violation of a primary IC — a classic latent ESD-diode wear / field-failure path.

The tell (why the RX side is fine and TX is not)

The identical “12 V step through a coupling cap” on the RX side is harmless because R30/R31 (22 kΩ) limit any clamp conduction to µA. The TX side simply lacks that series R — the protection was mirrored on RX but omitted on TX.

Fix — one part Insert a series resistor Rotx ≈ 1.5–2.2 kΩ between OUTP (pin 12) and C14: OUTP → Rotx → C14 → TALK_BRIDGE. It caps the worst-case clamp current at (12−4)/2.2k ≈ 3.6 mA and forms a ~72 Hz high-pass with C14 (harmless for voice ≥ 300 Hz). This mirrors the RX R30/R31 protection. Update DESIGN.md “Audio path” TX chain to match.
Calibration

The mechanism and the abs-max violation are certain; the peak current is moderated by the GAQ SSR's gradual optical turn-on (tens of mA, not amps), so the codec might survive in practice. But for a one-off where a respin costs weeks, a one-resistor fix is cheap insurance against a repeated-every-PTT overstress — fix before ordering.

W1 USB surge-clamp coordination: SMF5.0A clamp (~9.2 V) exceeds the TPD2S017 VCC tolerance

Found by: absmax:USB front-end refuter · SAFE-1 / SAFE-7 · DESIGN “Power tree”

D10 (SMF5.0A) clamps VBUS_F to ~9.2 V at rated surge. The ESD device D5 (TPD2S017) takes its VCC from VBUS_F and is recommended ≤ 5.5 V (its “6 V” is the VIO rating, not a VCC abs-max). On a sustained VBUS over-voltage (bad charger / surge) D5's VCC reaches ~9.2 V and — because its data-line clamps track VCC — pushes the USB lines toward ~10 V at the ESP USB pins (abs-max 3.6 V). For genuine ESD (ns transients on D±) the part works as intended; the gap is a sustained VBUS fault, ultimately contained by F1 (1 A) opening + the sacrificial-board policy (SAFE-7).

Action: accept under SAFE-7, or fit a tighter VBUS clamp if you want the USB front-end itself to survive a bad supply.

W2 Bus TVS surge margin is modest

Found by: derive:bus TVS refuter · SAFE-1 · DESIGN “SAFE-7 bus protection”

H24VND3BA: VC = 50 V @ 6 A (8/20 µs) is the last characterized point — only 10 V under the 60 V SSR off-state. Linear extrapolation of the two datasheet points reaches 60 V at ~9.3 A (1.56× rated). The +24 V standoff also sits only +7 V above the +17 V normal envelope. Fine for a low-energy 12 V bus, but little headroom if a stiff surge hits.

Action: accept for a one-off, or step to a higher-IPP / lower-clamp TVS for margin.

W3 Shared opto emitter R3 erodes the simultaneous-ring detect margin

Found by: derive:ring detection refuter · RING-2 / RING-4 · DESIGN “Bell/session sense front-end”

R3 (1 kΩ) is the shared emitter return for OC1 + OC2. In saturation each phototransistor sinks ~0.27 mA (not “µA” as DESIGN states). With both bells active at once — a front-door ring overlapping an apartment tone peak, which DESIGN confirms can happen — the shared node lifts to ~0.54 V, raising each output's LOW to ~0.63–0.91 V. At the pessimistic Vce(sat) + 3V3 +5 % corner that touches/crosses the ESP VIL (0.825 V), risking a missed/marginal detection.

Fix (cheap): give each opto its own emitter resistor, or tie the emitters straight to GND (R3 isn't required for the sense). The DESIGN claim “R_em carries only µA / immaterial” is inaccurate.

W4 RX rising-edge abs-max margin is thin (safe), and the coupling caps are DC-bias-asymmetric

Found by: derive:RX front-end refuter · AUDIO-6 / AUDIO-8 · DESIGN “Audio path” RX

DESIGN names the call-end fall as the worst MIC-pin case; the real worst is the rising edge (power-up 0→12.1 V, or the call-end snap-back to +13.9 V), coupling to MIC1P ≈ 3.12–3.46 V — within the +3.6 V abs-max but only 0.14–0.37 V margin (vs the ~1.95 V the design implies). It stays safe because R30/R31 (22 kΩ) current-limit any clamp conduction to µA — so this is a margin/documentation note, not a damage path. Separately, C16 sits at +12 V DC bias while C17 sits at 0 V; even as matched 50 V X5R parts the DC-bias derating makes them slightly unequal, marginally degrading 50/100 Hz hum rejection — already on the bench-gate list.

Action: bench-verify hum/levels (already planned); optionally tighten the worst-case note in DESIGN.

Method & baseline

The blind protocol

Each blind agent received only: a claim-free slice of the exported KiCad netlist (connectivity + values), the relevant datasheets, and the measured bus envelope from osci/ — plus one narrow, neutral question. None received DESIGN.md, REQUIREMENTS.md, the sim assertions, or any statement of intended behaviour or expected answer. They derived what the circuit does from first principles; the orchestrator held the claims and compared. Divergence = a candidate pre-fab bug. Every high-impact conclusion was then handed to a second agent prompted to refute it — find the operating point, tolerance corner, or fault where it fails. A claim is corroborated only if the refuter couldn't break it.

Measured bus envelope used (claim-free, from osci/)

P1 = common = GND. P2 idles +12.1 V, sags to ~9.4 V under a call, dips to ~2.6 V at call-end, peak +13.9 V (one +15.1 V glitch). P3 idle ~0 V high-Z; door-open bridges P2↔P3 at ~7.1–7.7 V. P4 (Türruf) idle 0 V → ~9.25 V pedestal (onset overshoot ~+11.1 V) for the ~60 s call. P5 (Etagenruf) DC-free tone, +7.8 / −5 V (one −11 V glitch). Overall normal envelope ≈ −11 … +17 V.
