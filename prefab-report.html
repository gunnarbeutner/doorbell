Klingel V4 — pre-fab blind verification

Gate report · ESP32 (ESPHome) interface board for the TCS/STR TV20/S bus, WF26 replacement · Generated: Thu Jul 2 11:24:46 CEST 2026

GO-WITH-FIXES

Thirteen design claims were gated against blind re-derivations, adversarial refutations, and a coverage audit. The electrical design is fundamentally sound and exceptionally well corroborated: every blind agent independently reproduced the power chain, RX/TX audio topology, ring-sense polarity, door break-before-make, default-safe boot states, TVS sizing, and full pinout/polarity maps from the netlist, raw bus captures (re-computed from ~48M samples), and local datasheets — no pin swap, no reversed part, no hard abs-max break in powered operation, no refutation above warning severity. Three things stop an unconditional GO: (1) the coverage audit's blocker — mechanical fit (MECH-1/1a MUSTs, Z-height still TBD in DESIGN.md) is checked by nothing, and a plunger/height miss forces the respin this gate exists to prevent; (2) the SSR LED drive claim ("worst corner ~4.9 mA ≈ 2.5x operate") is falsified at guaranteed corners — all five LEDs land at 3.8–4.13 mA, below the SUPSiC 5 mA recommended floor, with K3/K4 only 1.27x over their 3.0 mA max operate current (the claim used the wrong 2 mA figure for the 412EH); (3) the door watchdog (DOOR-6 fail-safe) is provably dead at the stacked paper corner — at guaranteed VOH 2.64 V, R25=5.1 M charges Q4's gate at only ~27 nA against an 80 nA IGSS allowance and plateaus at 2.23 V, below the 2.5 V Vth max. All three have cheap pre-fab fixes. Remaining divergences (two formal ES8311 abs-max exceedances vs the '0 violations' claim, K5 pull-in paper margin, floor-call crosstalk onto OC1, three blocks with source-integrity dings) are genuine but current-limited, firmware-mitigated (masking verified present in doorbell-v4.yaml), or bench-settleable on a one-off board. Verdict: fix the three blockers, then fabricate.

Warnings 9 items on record

Abs-max sweep on every IC pin

The '0 violations expected' claim is falsified on paper by two formal ES8311 exceedances, both current-limited: (1) MIC1P unpowered (the supported passive-fallback state, AVDD=0, window +/-0.3 V) takes ~0.5 mA / tau ~23 ms injections through R30 22k on every bus step, hot-plug, and session release — the datasheet publishes no injection-current allowance to clear it against; (2) OUTP clamps at 3.3 V + BAT54S VF = 3.62–3.70 V (and -0.32..-0.40 V on the door-open collapse), ~0.1 V past the AVDD+0.3/AGND-0.3 window for ms at <=4.3 mA while K1 is on. Almost certainly survivable (that is what clamps do), but the claim overstates; note also the blind analyst's envelope (13.9 V) understated the true capture record (+17.18 V P2, +15.5 V P4) — the CLAIM's own ~-11..+17 V envelope was right.

SSR LED drive / door delay — Q3 corner

K2's operation cannot be proven from guaranteed numbers: Q3 (2N7002) gate high level has only 0.14 V of enhancement at the VOH floor 2.64 V vs Vth max 2.5 V, and the datasheet guarantees no Rds/ID below VGS=5 V. Typ Vth 1.6 V works fine; bench-verify K2 make on the real board (the blocker-3 R25/C20 fix does not touch this leg). Also: the GAQY412EH Ton/Toff event mapping in the blind analysis was swapped — worst-case K4 break latency is 2.0 ms, not 1.5 ms (break-before-make margin still >=15 ms, conclusion intact); and a stuck-high DOOR_DRV leaves K4 open (passive latch path severed) until the MCU recovers — the claim already acknowledges this as harmless.

Ring detection — cross-channel selectivity (RING-4)

Refuter-verified from raw captures: the floor-call tone couples onto P4 at ~85% amplitude through the board's own chime path (K3 Ron + C19/C21), giving OC1 a ~680 Hz low-pulse burst with ~2x drive margin over the VIL threshold — the house-ring GPIO fires on every floor call at the hardware level, and auto-open hangs off OC1. I verified doorbell-v4.yaml's OC1 chain is a plain delayed_on: 50ms, which a 0.1–0.5 ms pulse train with 1–2 ms gaps cannot satisfy, and OC2 is stretch-filtered + masked by house_doorbell/PTT — so RING-4 holds, but it is firmware-load-bearing: bench-confirm no phantom house-ring/auto-open during a floor call (LTV-217 toff stretch at the 10k load must stay below the inter-pulse gaps).

K5 ring-latch pull-in margin

Paper corner: G6K must-operate is 9.6 V (80% of 12 V) vs the 9.2–9.4 V measured ring pedestal. Refuters largely defused it — the pedestal was measured with the old WF26's ~320R coil loading the line; V4's 1315R coil sees the near-unloaded drive (our-ring-p4-floating: ~399 ms continuously above 10.6 V, peak 11.9 V), and the Omron sample-distribution graph shows real coils operating at ~59–65% at 23 C — but it remains a guaranteed-corner gap on the passive core's central function. Bench-verify pull-in + 60 s seal-in hold against the live TV20/S at Stage bring-up (VERIFICATION).

Footprint / pinout — D6 (source integrity: sourceVerified=false)

The blind block asserted D6 polarity 'correct' without citing the KT-0603R datasheet, which actually numbers pin 1 = ANODE — opposite the PCM_JLCPCB pad1=cathode convention the symbol uses (verified: D6 = PCM_JLCPCB:D_0603 in doorbell.kicad_pcb). Wrong orientation = status LED dark (3.3 V reverse is within the 5 V VR, no damage). Not resolvable from repo files — eyeball D6's orientation in the JLCPCB assembly preview / first article. Every other pinout on the board was independently re-verified clean.

Abs-max sweep (source integrity: sourceVerified=false)

The blind analyst rated K3's chime current against '0.5 A cont / 1.5 A 100 ms', but the GAQY412E_EH datasheet prints IPeak 0.6 A/100 ms — the 1.5 A figure is untraceable (GAQW212GS's or recalled from the Panasonic AQY family). Verdict survives (re-derived worst-case gong/inrush ~34–74 mA, under both numbers), but the block leaned on a 2.5x-too-generous rating; per the source-integrity rule its margin claims should be re-read against the printed 0.6 A.

Datasheet conformance (source integrity: sourceVerified=false) + power corners

Three linked paper deviations: (1) the U4 VIN headroom check used an uncited SS14 VF — with the printed 0.55 V max, LP5907 VIN dips below its 'VOUT + 1 V' guaranteed-PSRR condition whenever VBUS sags under ~4.9 V during WiFi-TX bursts, so audio-rail rejection is unguaranteed exactly when supply noise peaks (few-dB SNR risk only, regulation unaffected); (2) SGM2212 COUT is ~20.3 uF nominal vs the 1–10 uF ROC ceiling (~10–12 uF after DC-bias derating; App Info says larger low-ESR COUT helps — paper-only deviation, but it pulls against Espressif's 22 uF ask); (3) ES8311 Note 3 ('VDDD must be on when VDDA is on') is transiently violated for ms at every unplug (DVDD collapses before the lightly-loaded AVDD) — a recommendation breach with no abs-max crossed, flagged independently by two refuters.

Power chain — accepted oddities

Corroborated clean overall, three benign notes to keep on record: J3 parallels J1 upstream of F1 with no ORing element (two live 5 V sources back-drive one — acceptable on a one-off, do not power both); D10's 5.0 V standoff sits nominally below a 5.25 V high-line USB source (leakage-only, VBR min 6.4 V); U2 is the thermal hot spot (~0.7 W, TJ ~107 C) only under an unrealistic sustained 100%-duty WiFi-TX — real duty is far lower, but the copper pour under the SOT-223 was never checked (see blind spots).

Audio performance has no pass/fail numbers (coverage audit)

AUDIO-1/2/6 carry no level, SNR, pop-amplitude, or turnaround numbers, so the RX/TX blocks could only ever verify abs-max, never 'good enough': door-station speech level vs ADC noise floor, TX level vs the WF26 mic reference (must stay 'talk', never read as OeT), and the K1-make +12 V-step pop through C14 onto the shared speech pair are all bench-gated with nothing quantitative to gate against. Adopt the audit's suggested AUDIO-10-style specs into REQUIREMENTS.md before bench bring-up so Stage testing has criteria.
