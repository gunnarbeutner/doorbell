Coverage & blind spots — what this verdict is NOT evidence about

This GO-WITH-FIXES verdict certifies what the 13 blind blocks independently re-derived from netlist + datasheets + the measured bus envelope. It is not evidence about the dimensions below: they were out of scope for the blind derivation (layout/copper properties, AC transfer functions, thermal, EMI, surge energy beyond the part rating, and several conclusions resting on absent primary datasheets). Several of these are explicitly bench-settable on the assembled board.

Differential surge stacking across the P2↔P3 SSRs (K1/K2) vs the 60 V VL
Bus-TVS block verifies single-line standoff/clamp but the per-line TVS do not clamp the P2↔P3 differential; a simultaneous opposite-polarity differential surge would stack two clamps (70–100 V) across the open SSR, exceeding 60 V. Surge energy/current adequacy of the bus TVS vs a real lightning/EFT event
H24VND3BA VC=50 V is specified only up to its 6 A rating; the osci captures contain only normal traffic, so the real fault current the TCS bus can deliver into a clamping TVS, and behavior beyond 6 A, are not in any primary source.
PCB-layout-dependent items: decoupling-cap proximity, ES8311 single-point (star) grounding, per-pin HF decoupling assignment
All blind work was netlist + datasheet + osci-envelope based; placement/proximity ('as close as possible'), the AGND/DGND/EP star ground, and which 100 nF serves which IC are layout/copper properties invisible in connectivity. These were out of scope for the blind derivation and are not certified by this review.
Datasheet absences (LTV-217-B-G low-current CTR curve, 2N7002 Vgs(th), SS14 Vf, FB1 low-freq inductance, H24VND3BA/SMF5.0A primary PDFs)
Several conclusions rest on LCSC parametric strings / symbol descriptions / family-typical values rather than a directly-read primary datasheet (no local PDFs for these parts).
