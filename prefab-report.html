Blockers — 4 must-fix items before fabrication

Blocker Door opener timing & watchdog

Issue

The max-on watchdog one-shot R25(3M)*C20(2.2uF) can release K2 BEFORE the firmware door pulse ends. C20 = CL10A225KO8NNNC is a Class-2 X5R ceramic (confirmed at kicad/doorbell.kicad_sch line 23680/23735); X5R DC-bias + temperature derating drops effective C to ~1.4-1.6uF, and with 2N7002 Q4 Vgs(th) at the datasheet minimum 1.0V the trip time falls to ~1.52-1.73s. The firmware door pulse is a fixed 1750ms (firmware/doorbell-v4.yaml: front_door_buzzer output GPIO10/DOOR_DRV, duration:1750ms). At the fast corner the watchdog opens the P2<->P3 bridge ~50-230ms early, shortening or failing a legitimate door-open. The blind refutation independently broke this (broke:true, warning) and my own RC sweep reproduces the 1.52-1.73s trip.

Fix

Restore worst-case margin between the watchdog window and the 1.75s pulse. Cheapest: increase R25 (e.g. 3M -> 5.6-6.8M) and/or move C20 to a C0G/X7R part with low DC-bias derating so the fast-corner trip stays comfortably above ~2.5s; alternatively reduce the firmware pulse below the guaranteed fast-corner trip. Re-run the corner sweep (Vgs(th) 1.0-2.5V x C derating x R/C tolerance) and require the fast corner > pulse + margin and the slow corner < a stated upper bound.

Blocker RX audio front-end vs codec abs-max

Issue

No block and no requirement verifies the OTHER end of the RX path: that a nominal door-station voice on line 2, after the 22k/3.3k = -18dB divider (R30/R33) plus VMID bias, lands at a usable code level above the ADC noise floor. Every block asks 'within abs-max?' not 'above noise floor / at usable level?'. An attenuator sized only for the +-8.8V gong abs-max headroom can leave normal speech 30-40dB down in the ADC, failing intelligibility (AUDIO-6). DESIGN itself flags RX trim as bench-gated/OPEN.

Fix

Add a two-sided RX check and a numeric requirement (e.g. RX-1: nominal door-station speech reaches the ES8311 ADC at >= -20 dBFS with >= 40 dB in-band SNR). Bench-measure delivered ADC level at the divider and set the codec PGA/ALC; if the passive -18dB divider leaves too little headroom, the divider ratio is the trim point. Settle on the bench before relying on the board.
