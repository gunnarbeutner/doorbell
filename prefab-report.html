NO-GO

The electrical design is broadly coherent and automated checks cover passive fallback, door safety, ring detection, TX isolation, gong rejection and protection topology. Two V4.2 ordering gates remain: the real TV20/S must accept the new RC-ramped talk handshake, and K3 must not reclose early after reset or brownout while trapped chime charge remains.

Top three actions

  1. Perform and document the real-bus V4.2 passive-split/ramped-handshake gate.
  2. Complete K3's reset/brownout-safe reclose contract and its regression.
  3. Bench the highest-value remaining margins together: floor-call capacitor/diode stress, K5 pull-in, and RX/TX/hum/onset levels.

Blockers

BLOCKER

V4.2 TX handshake

The new R34/C25+C26/R35 leg has an approximately 25 ms assertion ramp. Simulation proves its topology and gong rejection, but cannot establish that the real TV20/S talk detector accepts the ramp or that forwarded audio has the required level.

Required fix: run the TODO's non-invasive passive-split live-bus test. Require forwarded audio, gong rejection and talk assertion. Use the documented high-impedance buffered alternative if assertion fails.

BLOCKER

K3 reset/brownout contract

R36 and factory-bridged JP2 provide the intended roughly 2.35 s bleed, and simulation proves safe reclose after 5 tau. An ESP reset or brownout nevertheless releases MUTE_DRV and closes normally-closed K3 immediately.

Required fix: enforce line-4 idle plus a full approximately 12 s discharge before reclose, including reset/brownout during the interval, and add regression coverage for the chosen behavior.

Warnings

Power and stability margins

  • Effective U2/LP5907 capacitance and ESR under DC bias are not established.
  • D10 has little stand-off margin to high USB VBUS.
  • Full simultaneous source/load and enclosure thermal margins remain unclosed.

Ring/session margins

  • LTV-217 B-rank CTR is guaranteed at 5 mA, while measured LED currents are about 1.25-1.9 mA.
  • K5 pull-in should be checked at the actual V4.2 loaded P4 voltage and temperature.

Door timing

  • C18 is not diode-rearmed; rapid reassertion reduces make delay. Firmware's 0.5 s minimum off-time is therefore load-bearing.
  • The 10 MΩ watchdog node is sensitive to leakage and nominal component corners.

Audio and codec

  • Unpowered ES8311 clamp injection is not rated.
  • RX/TX level, hum, cold-start onset and smart-TX quality remain bench-calibration questions.
  • The ES8311 grounding/layout guide is not followed literally; DVDD bead and optional I2C filters are absent.

Protection and polarity

  • Opposite-polarity per-line TVS clamping could exceed a 60 V line-to-line PhotoMOS rating; surge environment is not characterized.
  • C19/C21 anti-series electrolytics intentionally reverse-bias one can per half-cycle, but the exact RVT PDF gives no reverse-bias qualification.
  • Verify D6 CPL rotation; failure would only leave the status LED dark.

GPIO drive evidence

  • Typical SSR LED currents are healthy and operate thresholds have margin.
  • The ESP32 module's 40 mA drive value is typical and loaded VOH is not guaranteed by the cited table; the 5 mA recommended-current floor is therefore not formally closed.
  • The C20/D11 discharge transient into DOOR_DRV is unbounded by a series resistor.

Corroborated design claims

Connectivity and safe defaults

No verified pin swap was found. USB routing is intact; unpowered defaults preserve passive operation and keep talk/door outputs open.

TX isolation and gong filter

K1 structurally isolates codec TX at idle. The V4.2 low-pass strongly rejects gong-band energy, and simulator coverage includes passive-microphone isolation in both K3 states.

K3 hardware mitigation

R36+JP2 exist. Tests reproduce the unsafe JP2-cut case and verify safe reclose after 5 tau when bridged.

Mechanical fit

V4.1 fit the real enclosure and actuated both switches. This remains valid while the outline, bosses, button geometry, transducer/wire entry and height envelope stay unchanged.
