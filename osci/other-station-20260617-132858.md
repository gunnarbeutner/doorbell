# other-station-20260617-132858 — a neighbour's ring, seen on our bus

A passive scope capture of the STR bus while **another apartment's handset received a ring**
(not ours). It's the cleanest reference we have for what a door call looks like on the shared
bus, captured at a high enough sample rate to resolve the gong tones.

## What it shows / confirms

- **Line 4 is address-selective.** IN_P4 (our line 4) stays cold the whole time (mean ≈ 0 V, no
  DC level) — this is the *neighbour's* ring, so our line 4 is never driven. Our OC1 (on IN_P4)
  would not see it.
- **Line 2 is the shared party line.** The gong and the listen window ride **P2**, which we tap
  even though the call isn't ours.
- **The DC sag is the neighbour's relay coil.** P2 dropping 12.05 → 9.44 V (−2.61 V) is the
  latched WF26_K1 coil (~320 Ω to common) loading the shared line against its ~85 Ω source
  impedance. The loudspeaker is C1-coupled (DC-blocked), so it carries the gong *AC* but does
  not move the DC level.
- **Door-open is a P2↔P3 bridge.** The neighbour's door-release shorts the shared pair (both
  lines meet at ≈7.7 V) — visible on our wires too.
- **The gong tones are real at last.** At the earlier 5 kSa/s the gong read as 841/1009 Hz
  (aliased/smeared); at 25 kSa/s it resolves to **≈1010 Hz then ≈672 Hz**. (A third tone at the
  very onset, before ~3.8 s, may be masked by the coil-engage transient.)

## AC components — IN_P4 and P3

Band-resolved AC (volts RMS), split into the mains-pickup band (<300 Hz) and the gong band
(0.6–3.5 kHz), with P2 for scale:

| Channel | Phase | Vpp | total AC | mains <300 Hz | gong 0.6–3.5 kHz | dominant |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **IN_P4** | idle | 1.34 | 0.336 | 0.329 | 0.017 | 50 Hz |
| | gong | 2.14 | 0.393 | 0.316 | 0.120 | 50, 1011 |
| | door | 2.79 | 0.532 | 0.280 | 0.017 | 50 (+ broadband) |
| **P3** | idle | 0.55 | 0.057 | 0.008 | 0.019 | 50/150/250 |
| | gong | 0.97 | 0.078 | 0.007 | 0.036 | 1011, 3032 |
| | door | 9.85 | 4.033 | 0.167 | 0.030 | step edges |
| *P2 (ref)* | gong | 9.38 | 0.744 | 0.020 | 0.411 | 1011, 3032 |

- **IN_P4 is essentially a mains-hum antenna.** Its AC is ~0.33 V RMS (1.3 Vpp), **~98 % of it
  50 Hz mains pickup**, present constantly — the signature of a **high-impedance, undriven node**
  (our line 4 isn't driven during a neighbour's ring). The gong crosstalks in only weakly
  (0.12 V), and the door-open adds a broadband switching transient. **No DC rides it** → OC1
  (which detects the ~12 V Türruf *DC level*) won't false-trigger on this sub-volt, DC-free hum:
  the address-selectivity holds right through to the sense front-end.
- **P3 is quiet, with two intrusions.** Idle ≈ 0.057 V RMS (lower impedance than IN_P4, barely
  any hum). During the gong it sees a small bleed — **0.036 V (~9 % of P2)** at 1011/3032 Hz. The
  big event is the door-open: **Vpp ≈ 9.85 V, 4.0 V RMS** — P3 yanked into the P2↔P3 bridge (the
  40–90 Hz "tones" are just the step edges, not real tones).
- **Gong coupling ranking:** P2 0.411 → IN_P4 0.120 (29 %) → P3 0.036 (9 %). Loudest on the
  shared listen line P2; crosstalks onto the floating high-Z IN_P4 more than onto the lower-Z P3.
- **Design read:** the IN_P4 hum is a high-Z sense-node artifact (DC-free → no false rings, as
  the firmware DC-debounce assumes); the P3 gong-bleed + door transient land only on the TX line,
  which the board drives/listens on only during *our own* PTT — never during a neighbour's call —
  so both are benign.

## Takeaways

- **Capture the gong at ≥ 25 kSa/s** — 5 kSa/s is too slow and aliases the chime.
- This is the reference for what the codec's **RX tap on line 2** will hear during any call on
  the bus (ours or a neighbour's).
