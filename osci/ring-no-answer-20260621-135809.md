## Findings

**This is *our* ring, and it was answered — but the door was never opened.** All the *our-door*
markers are present: line 4 itself goes hot (9.25 V, not just a bleed onto P2), the gong is **on
line 4**, and a session seals in and holds. What's **missing** is the door-open. In `ring-…195221`
and `ring4-…183218` the session ends with **P3 jumping 0 → ~7–9 V and a P2↔P3 bridge**; here P3
stays cold (max 0.05 V) and there is **no bridge** — instead the whole tied P2/line-4 pair collapses
together and P2 recovers in ~110 ms. That is a **plain seal-in release** (hang-up / call timeout),
not a door-release transfer. The discriminator is P3: a door-open shows on P3, and **P3 is flat
through the entire 100 s**. → ring answered, opener never fired.

**The end is a release, not a door-open — confirmed on three counts:**
- **P3 never rises.** A door-open is a P2↔P3 short pulling P3 up to ~7 V; here P3's DC never leaves
  the noise floor (≤0.05 V).
- **P2 is *driven* low — the TV20/S pulls P2, not line 4.** The tied pair drops together (CH2 stays
  ~0.18 V above CH1 through the fall), but the tell is *after* separation: **P2 holds a ~2.8 V plateau
  for ~18 ms while line 4 is already down near 0**, then snaps back to 12 V (~112 ms total). A *held*
  low = P2 actively driven — were line 4 the driven node, P2 would recover the instant K5 releases; P2
  also **leads** line 4 at the fall onset by ~60–100 µs. So the latch drops via a **brief P2-low
  pulse** (the K5 coil loses its P2 supply), not a P2↔P3 door-open short (slow ~1.5 s ramp,
  `door-opener-…165705`).
- **No bridge meet-point.** P2 and P3 never converge to a common ~7 V level.

**Tone lags the line-4 pedestal — the reactive-suppress window is here too.** At 50 kSa/s the
onset resolves: the line-4 **pedestal** crosses 1 V at **31250.0 ms** and 8 V at **31255.9 ms**;
an **edge transient** rides the step at ~31249–31251 ms, then the AC envelope **falls back to the
noise floor** (~0.06 V) for ~31251–31262 ms before the **gong tone proper ramps in at ~31263–31265
ms** and holds. So the audible chime starts **~13–15 ms after** the pedestal begins — the same
~10–15 ms gap noted in `ring4`. Enough to suppress the gong **reactively** (a relay's ~5 ms
pull-in just fits; a PhotoMOS fits easily) — chime-suppress does **not** need pre-arming.

**Gong:** **~1010 Hz** fundamental with a strong **3030 Hz** 3rd harmonic at onset, descending
through **~840 Hz** to **~672 Hz** (the 3-Klang, a falling sequence), decaying over ~3.9 s — matches
the 1010 / 3032 / 672 Hz chime of `ring-…195221` and `other-station-…132858`.

**Session geometry matches the handset seal-in model.** Line 4 holds at **9.25 V**, **0.18 V below
P2 (9.43 V)** — line 4 is pulled up *from* P2 through the latched contact, not driven by the
TV20/S, so it tracks just under P2 (the same 0.1–0.2 V offset as the other held sessions). P2's
~2.67 V sag (12.11 → 9.43 V) is the latched coil loading the shared line. The session simply runs
its course (~58.5 s) and releases — no door in the loop.
