## What it shows / confirms

- **Pure P2↔P3 operation — line 4 untouched.** IN_P4 stays flat at ~0 V (±0.4 V noise floor) the
  whole time, with **no coupling even at the switching edges**. The door-opener is exactly the
  TV20/S test *"Klemmen 2 u. 3 brücken,"* nothing on line 4 — validating **K2 = a direct P2↔P3
  short**.
- **It's a dead short, not a resistive bridge.** P2 and P3 are forced to the *same* level
  (7.14 vs 7.18 V). A 2.2 kΩ bridge (the talk handshake) would leave them *separated*; equal means
  a true short. So the door path is cleanly distinguished from the talk path — the practical side of
  the "short fires the opener, 2.2 kΩ doesn't" threshold.
- **The meet point (~7.16 V) is impedance-divided, not midpoint.** From 12 V and 0 V landing at
  7.16 V ⇒ P2's source is ~1.5× stiffer than P3's pull-to-0 (R3/R2 ≈ 1.5).
- **Asymmetric recovery (new):** the short yanks P2 *down* fast (low-Z), but P2 climbs *back* slowly
  (~1.5 s, τ ≈ 0.5 s) through its own bias-source resistance into the bus capacitance. So **after a
  door-open, line 2 takes ~1.5 s to settle back to 12 V** — the codec's RX tap would see a slow DC
  drift upward for ~1.5 s following any door-open (relevant to RX timing / AGC).
- **No audio.** Standalone open (no ring) ⇒ pure DC bridge + switching transients at the make/break
  edges; nothing tonal.

## Caveat

This is the **trigger waveform** as seen on our bus, **not** the opener *firing*. The actual
8–12 V AC solenoid current is switched at the TV20/S / door end, off our tap — to confirm the door
physically released you'd need to hear/see it (or scope the opener pair).
