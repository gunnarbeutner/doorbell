# p5-chime-20260621-165156 — Etagenruf (floor call): P5 driven, C1-coupled onto P4, common-mode artifact on P2/P3

An **apartment/floor-call (Etagenruf)** capture with **only the real WF26 handset + the V3 board**
on the bus — **no V4 hardware attached**. It pins the floor-call tone's **peak voltage** (for
bus-TVS sizing), shows the tone reaching line 4 through the handset's **C1**, and — the key
correction — establishes that the chime visible on lines 2/3 is **not a real bus signal** but a
shared-ground (common-mode) measurement artifact. It also predicts the V4 OC1/OC2 response and
root-causes the V3 board's dead Etagenruf sense.

## Findings

**The Etagenruf is on P5 — DC-free, ~+7.8 V peak, a buzzy 681 Hz tone.** Line 5 carries the
floor-call tone (gated onto P5 by the ET button; the TV20/S central unit never sees it).
**DC-free** (mean +0.09 V), peaks **+7.8 V / −5 V**. Spectrally a **681 Hz fundamental + harmonics
2–5** (1362 / 2043 / 2724 / 3405 Hz), but only ~20 % of the AC power sits in those five lines — the
rest is in sharp edges above 3.4 kHz, so it is a **buzzy/impulsive** waveform (crest factor ~13),
which is why it sounds rough. (The tool's 18.77 V Vpp is inflated by a single −10.97 V sample; the
robust envelope is +7.8 / −5 V.)

**The tone reaches line 4 through the handset's C1.** P4 carries the *same* chime at **~85 %**
amplitude (peak +7.1 V, in-phase with P5), coupled through the handset's **C1 (22 µF, P4↔P5)** —
~11 Ω at 681 Hz, a near-direct AC tie. DC-free. (V4 reproduces this cap as C19.)

**The chime on lines 2/3 is a shared-P1 common-mode artifact — *not* a real bus signal.** P2 and
P3 show the chime too, but ~20 dB down (~60 mV), and it is **not** a coupling onto those lines:

- **Flat spectrum** (P2/P5 & P3/P5 harmonic ratios flat ~0.10; HF/LF tilt 0.7–0.8) — capacitive
  crosstalk would tilt ∝ f.
- **Equal on P2 and P3** despite P2 being a low-Z 12 V rail and P3 high-Z — rules out a per-line,
  impedance-dependent coupling.
- **Phase:** P2≡P3 (Δ ≈ 1°, in-phase) and **180° anti-phase to P5**; P4 is in-phase with P5 (its
  real C1 coupling).

That is the signature of **common-impedance coupling**: the Etagenruf's LS1 return current drops
voltage along the shared **P1** conductor, shifting the reference for every P1-grounded probe
(anti-phase because it is the *return*-current drop). It requires nothing at the TV20/S — purely a
local return-current effect. **Confirm by** moving the scope's P1 ground point or taking P2
differentially (the artifact shifts/vanishes). → **Lines 2/3 are clean of the Etagenruf;** only P5
(driven) and P4 (via C1) carry it.

**Peak voltages (feed the bus-TVS sizing).** Robust (single-sample glitches dropped):

| Line | DC | Peak (pos / neg) |
|---|---|---|
| P5 (driven) | ~0 | +7.8 / −5 V |
| P4 (via C1) | ~0 | +7.1 / −8.5 V |
| P2 (12 V rail) | +12.07 | +13.9 V (one +15.1 glitch) |
| P3 (hi-Z) | ~0 | ±5 V (mains hum) |

Highest *sustained* level is **P2 at ~14 V** (12 V rail + ring transients), not the Etagenruf — so
a bidirectional **18 V-standoff** bus TVS (e.g. SMF18CA) clears every line with margin, and **P2 is
the binding line**. (See `../TODO.md` bus-protection.)

**V4 sense prediction — OC2 fires, OC1 stays silent.** Applying the V4 front-end (per-opto 5.1 kΩ
limiter + 10 kΩ pull-up + shared 1 kΩ emitter → effective conduction threshold ~1.6–3.7 V over
CTR 0.5–2.6) to these waveforms:

- **OC2 (P5) detects.** The buzzy peaks cross the threshold once per cycle (max gap 2.7 ms even at
  a 5 V threshold), so `delayed_off:50ms` stretches them into a continuous level over the ~470 ms
  burst and `delayed_on:150ms` latches — correct floor-call detection.
- **OC1 (P4) does *not* fire**, despite the ~85 % C1-coupled chime. P4 is **DC-free** (mean
  +0.02 V), so OC1's LED toggles at audio rate with a max *continuous* conduction of **0.58 ms** —
  far short of the **continuous 50 ms** its `delayed_on:50ms` needs. Discriminator: **DC pedestal
  (Türruf) vs DC-free AC (Etagenruf via C1)** — same opto toggling, opposite filter.

**This capture root-causes the V3 board's dead Etagenruf sense.** On the deployed V3 board the
apartment (Etagenruf) sense no longer fires while the house (Türruf) sense works — the fingerprint
of the **shared-limiter reverse-cook**. V3 used one **shared 5.1 kΩ** in the common opto-cathode
return, so a Türruf (line 4 → ~12 V) lifts the shared node to ~10.8 V and **reverse-biases the idle
Etagenruf LED past its 6 V V_R** — with the reverse current returning through line 5's 16 Ω speaker,
**bypassing the 5.1 kΩ**, limited only by the line-4 source impedance (tens of mA, sustained for
the ~60 s session, on the *frequent* house ring). That cooked the Etagenruf opto; the Türruf opto,
seeing only the milder/brief AC self-reverse, survived. (The LED clearly avalanches below ~11 V.)
**V4 fixes the mechanism with per-opto limiters**; **D9 is retained**. See `../DESIGN.md` "V3" and
"Bell / session sense front-end".

## Notes

- **No V4 hardware on the bus** — so the C1 coupling onto P4 is the *handset's* C1 (not V4's C19),
  and the P2/P3 artifact is the shared-P1 ground of the V3/scope tap, not any V4 part.
- P3's idle 50 Hz hum (~0.60 V RMS) collapses to ~0.04 V during the burst — a side-curiosity (the
  floating antenna's pickup changing), **not** central-unit re-termination (an earlier mis-read);
  it does not bear on the conclusions.
- Times are within the 5 s displayed window; the full acquisition was ~50 s, trimmed to the
